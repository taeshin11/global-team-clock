Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2, 2026

1. Overview

Global Team Clock ("we," "our," or "the Service") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains in plain language what information is collected when you use our website at global-team-clock.vercel.app, how that information is used, and what rights you have over it. We encourage you to read this document in full. If you have any questions, our contact information is provided at the end.

The Service is a free, browser-based timezone overlap dashboard. Because all core functionality runs locally in your browser, we have intentionally minimized our data footprint. We do not operate a user database, we do not require registration, and we do not store your timezone or scheduling configurations on any server we control.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit the Service, your browser makes standard HTTP requests to load the page. Vercel, our hosting provider, may log basic server-side information including your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URL, and the date and time of each request. This is standard web-server logging behavior and is not specific to our Service. We do not access or process these server logs for user profiling. Please refer to Vercel's Privacy Policy for details on how they handle hosting data.

The Service uses your browser's built-in Intl API to detect your local timezone. This detection happens entirely within your browser — no timezone query is sent to our servers or any third-party server.

2.2 Data Stored Locally in Your Browser

The Service uses your browser's localStorage to persist two small pieces of information between visits:

  • Language preference: The interface language you have selected (e.g., "en" for English, "ko" for Korean). This prevents you from needing to reselect your language each visit.
  • Visitor counter: A simple numeric counter that increments each time you visit. This is entirely local to your device and is never transmitted to us or anyone else.

You can clear all localStorage data at any time through your browser's settings (typically under "Site Data" or "Cookies and Site Data"). No personal information is contained in this data.

2.3 URL Parameters

When you use the "Copy Share Link" feature, your current configuration — including city selections and working hour settings — is encoded directly into the URL. This URL data is processed only by the browser of anyone who opens the link. It is not transmitted to or stored on our servers. Sharing a link shares only the configuration, not any personal information about you.

3. Cookies and Third-Party Advertising

The Service itself does not set first-party tracking cookies. However, we display advertisements through third-party advertising networks to support the free operation of the Service. These advertising partners may set their own cookies and use similar tracking technologies to serve relevant ads. Below is a description of each advertising partner and their data practices.

3.1 Google AdSense

We participate in Google AdSense, operated by Google LLC (or Google Ireland Limited for users in the European Economic Area). Google AdSense uses cookies, web beacons, and related technologies to serve advertisements based on your browsing history and inferred interests. Google may collect information about your visits to this and other websites to personalize the ads you see. You can review Google's data practices in the Google Privacy Policy. To opt out of personalized advertising from Google, visit Google Ads Settings or install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. You may also use the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out tool.

3.2 Adsterra

We may also display advertisements from Adsterra Network. Adsterra is an online advertising platform that may use cookies and device identifiers to deliver targeted advertising and to measure ad performance. Adsterra does not collect personally identifiable information directly, but its cookies may be used to build an anonymous interest profile associated with your browser. For more information, review Adsterra's Privacy Policy. You may be able to opt out of Adsterra's interest-based ads through your browser's cookie settings or through industry opt-out mechanisms such as the Your Online Choices platform.

4. Information We Do Not Collect

To be explicit about what we do not do:

  • We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personally identifiable information unless you contact us voluntarily.
  • We do not track which cities you add to the dashboard on our servers.
  • We do not collect precise geolocation data.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share any user data with third parties for marketing purposes.
  • We do not run analytics services such as Google Analytics on this Service.

5. Your Rights Under GDPR (European Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent national laws:

  • Right of access: You may request confirmation of whether we process personal data about you and, if so, a copy of that data.
  • Right to rectification: You may request correction of inaccurate personal data.
  • Right to erasure: You may request deletion of your personal data where we hold any.
  • Right to object: You may object to processing of your personal data for direct marketing or based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to restrict processing: You may request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability: You may request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.

Because we do not collect personal data directly (beyond what third-party ad networks may collect), most of these rights apply primarily to your interactions with those third parties. To exercise rights related to Google AdSense or Adsterra data, please contact those providers directly. To exercise any rights in relation to data we might hold (such as an email you sent us), contact us at taeshinkim11@gmail.com.

6. Your Rights Under CCPA (California Users)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants you specific rights regarding your personal information. You have the right to know what personal information is collected about you, the right to request deletion of your personal information, and the right to opt out of the sale of your personal information. We do not sell personal information. To the extent that our third-party advertising partners use your data, you may opt out by following the links provided in Section 3 above. For CCPA-related requests, contact us at taeshinkim11@gmail.com.

7. Data Security

All data transmitted between your browser and the Service is protected by HTTPS encryption. Because we do not collect or store personal information on our own servers, the risk of a data breach affecting your personal information through this Service is minimal. However, no internet transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security of information transmitted via third-party advertising networks. We recommend reviewing the security practices of Google AdSense and Adsterra in their respective policies.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the EEA). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us immediately at the email address below so we can take appropriate action.
