Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a city?

Type a city name or timezone in the search bar at the top. You can search by city name (e.g., "Tokyo"), country name, or IANA timezone ID (e.g., "Asia/Tokyo"). Click on a result to add it. You can also click the popular city buttons below the search bar.

How many cities can I add?

You can add up to 10 cities at the same time. This keeps the timeline view readable and the tool performant.

How do I adjust working hours?

Each city row has "Start" and "End" dropdown menus for working hours. The default is 09:00-18:00. Change these to match each team member's actual work schedule. The green overlay on the timeline will update automatically.

What does the "Apply to All" button do?

It copies the first city's working hours to all other cities. Useful when everyone works the same schedule in their local time.

How does "Find Best Meeting Time" work?

The algorithm finds all 1-hour slots where all cities have overlapping work hours. It ranks them by preference (midday is preferred over early morning). If no full overlap exists, it suggests the "least bad" option where the most cities are in their work hours.

How do I share my configuration?

Click the "Copy Share Link" button. The URL automatically updates as you add/remove cities and change work hours. Anyone who opens the link will see the exact same configuration.

What do the colors on the timeline mean?

Soft yellow: Daytime hours (6:00-18:00)
Soft indigo: Nighttime hours (18:00-6:00)
Light green overlay: Working hours for that city
Bright green overlay: Hours where ALL cities' work hours overlap
Red line: Current time in that city

Does this handle Daylight Saving Time (DST)?

Yes! Global Team Clock uses the browser's built-in Intl API, which automatically handles DST transitions. The offsets shown are always current and accurate.

What about half-hour timezones like India (UTC+5:30)?

Fully supported. The tool handles all timezone offsets including +5:30 (India), +5:45 (Nepal), +9:30 (Central Australia), and others.

Do you collect any data?

Global Team Clock runs entirely in your browser. No timezone data or city selections are sent to any server. We use localStorage for language preferences and visitor counting. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Can I use this on mobile?

Yes, Global Team Clock is fully responsive and works on phones and tablets. On smaller screens, the city info and timeline stack vertically for better readability.

Is it free?

Yes, completely free. No sign-up required, no limitations, no premium tier.
